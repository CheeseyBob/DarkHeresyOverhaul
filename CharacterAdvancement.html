Character Advancement

Each different Advance has an XP cost and two associated Aptitudes. When you take an Advance, the amount of XP you spend depends on how many matching Aptitudes you have:

  • Both Aptitues: you spend half the written XP cost.
  • One Aptitue: you spend the written XP cost.
  • Neither Aptitue: you spend double the written XP cost.

There are three different kinds of Advance:

  • Characteristic Advances increase a Characteristic by 5. You can take this up to 5 times for each Characteristic, with the XP cost going up each time. The XP costs and associated Aptitudes are shown in the tables below.
  • Skill Advances increase the bonus for a Skill by 10, or aquire a non-basic skill at a +0 bonus. You can take this up to a +30 bonus for each skill. Skills, their XP costs and associated Aptitudes are shown in the tables below.
  • Talent Advances give your character the benefit of a Talent. Talents, their XP costs and associated Aptitudes are listed in the Talents section.

Advancement After Character Creation

During play, taking Advances should be justified by the character's actions in one of the following ways.

  • Experience: After the character uses a Characteristic or Skill, they can take an Advance for that Characteristic or Skill.
  • Inspiration: Before taking an action, you can spend a Fate Point to allow the character to take a Talent Advance and then use that Talent.
  • Training: The character can spend time training or studying. If they have access to the appropriate resources, a successful Long Action Test using the appropriate Characteristic allows them to take any Advance.
