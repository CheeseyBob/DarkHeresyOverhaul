Talents - Dark Heresy Overhaul Index

Talents

Talents are a particular kind of Aspect which can be permenantly attached to characters. They cannot be Overcome and they each have one of the following effects on the character with the Talent:

  • Grant a new action.
  • Enhance the effects of an action.
  • Give a +10 bonus to an Attack action in a certain circumstance (bonuses to other types of Action are covered by Skills).

Talent List

  • Brutal Charge: Charge with double the bonus damage.
  • Crushing Blow: Heavy Blow with double the bonus damage.
  • Strong Attacks: Bulky Melee Weapons use double your Strength Bonus for damage.
  • Sweeping Blow: Melee Attack (maximum hits 1) with an extra hit on a different target for each Degree of Success.
  • Fearless: Immune to Pinning and Fear.
  • [Melee Weapon] Mastery: Get a +10 bonus to Melee Attack with [Melee Weapon]
  • [Ranged Weapon] Mastery: Get a +10 bonus to Ranged Attack with [Ranged Weapon]
  • Wrestler: Get a +10 bonus to Grapple Attack.
  • Gunslinger: Get a +10 bonus to Ranged Attack when using two pistols.
  • Dual-Wielder: Get a +10 bonus to Melee Attack when using two weapons.
  • Furious Assault: Get a +10 bonus to Flurry of Blows when using two weapons.
  • Lightning Attack: Get a +10 bonus to Flurry of Blows.
  • Quick Dagger: Small bladed weapons get (+1|+2) hits.
  • Quick Blade: Standard bladed weapons get (+0|+1) hits.
  • Sprinter: Your maximum bonus Movement is three times your base Movement, rather than two times.
  • Quick Draw: Draw your weapon without spending an Action.
  • Leap Up: Stand without spending an Action.
  • Precision Strike: With a Small bladed weapon, Melee Attack with maximum hits reduced to 1 and a +1 bonus to damage for each Degree of Success.
  • Careful Shot: Get an extra +10 when applying a bonus from Aim.
  • Careful Strike: Get an extra +10 when applying a bonus from Bide.
  • Careful Aim: Get an extra +10 bonus from Extended Aim.
  • Sniper Aim: Bonuses from Aim persist if you take another Aim action.
  • Rapid Reload: Reload as a Secondary Action.
  • Sound Constitution: Get an extra Wound.
  • Effortless Dodge: Spend two Degrees of Success on a Dodge to not spend your Reaction.
  • Effortless Parry: Spend two Degrees of Success on a Parry to not spend your Reaction.
  • Agile Dodge: Spend Degrees of Success on a Dodge to move 1m per DoS, up to a maximum of your Movement.
  • Riposte: Spend Degrees of Success on a Parry to get a +10 bonus per DoS to Melee Attack.
  • Follow-Through: Spend Degrees of Success on a Melee Attack to get a +10 bonus per DoS to Melee Attack.
  • Assault Fire: Move twice and Ranged Attack during the movement.
  • Assassin Strike: Move twice and Melee Attack during the movement.
  • Assassin Roll: You can Move unimpeeded when Prone.
  • Independent Targeting: When wielding two ranged weapons, you can Ranged Attack against two targets, assigning hits from one weapon to the second target.
  • Fast Retargeting: You can Extended Fire against two targets, assigning each hit to either of the targets.
  • Blood Filters:You can selectively choose not to Resist Chems if you wish, in which case the Aspect is downgraded once per hour.
  • Language (Low Gothic): You can understand Low Gothic.
  • Language (High Gothic): You can understand High Gothic.
  • Peer (Administratum): Get +20 to all Fellowship and Influence tests when interacting with members of the Adeptus Administratum.

Trade Talents

Get a +30 bonus to any relevant action.
  • Agri Worker: Used to grow, care for, and harvest crops and animals.
  • Apothecary: Used to blend and prepare herbal remedies.
  • Archaeologist: Used to locate, examine, and analyse ancient ruins and artefacts.
  • Armourer: Used to design, upgrade, and forge weaponry, from personal arms to starship batteries.
  • Astrographer: Used to create two- and three-dimensional representations of stellar locations and Warp routes.
  • Cartographer: Used to take accurate measurements and turn them into maps.
  • Chymist: Used to create poisons, drugs, and a wide variety of other compounds.
  • Cryptographer: Used to create or decode complicated ciphers, codes, and other puzzles.
  • Cook: Used to cook meals and determine if scavenged food is edible.
  • Copyist: Used to swiftly copy text, illuminate manuscripts and forge written material.
  • Explorator: Used in the exploration of unknown stellar regions.
  • Linguist: Used to learn or decipher new languages, both spoken and written, and even create new ones in some cases.
  • Loremancer: Used to recount events in a variety of ways, from dry recitations or texts to epic operas or statuary.
  • Mason: Used to assess and construct buildings.
  • Merchant: Used to find, bargain and sell trade goods.
  • Miner: Used to extract minerals, maintain mines and identify common hazards.
  • Morticator: Used to prepare, preserve, and often render down corpse remains into ingredients for a variety of preparations.
  • Performer: Used to perform for audiences in a variety of entertaining art forms, often using dance, song, and poetry.
  • Prospector: Used to find and identify valuable minerals.
  • Scrimshaw: Used to inscribe patterns, text, and art onto a variety of materials.
  • Sculptor: Used to create inspiring works of art in stone, metal, and other materials, often as part of Imperial edifices.
  • Shipwright: Used to design, upgrade, and manufacture void-capable vessels.
  • Soothsayer: Used to give the appearance of telling the future.
  • Survivalist: Used to scavenge and create primitive items from scavenged materials.
  • Swindler: Used to aquire wealth through trickery and deception.
  • Technomat: Used to maintain and repair technological devices, but through rote memorization rather than true understanding or comprehension.
  • Valet: Used to refine the appearance, give droll asides and tend to the needs of superiors in a gentlemanly fashion.
  • Voidfarer: Used in the day-to-day operation, logistics, and defence of starships.
  • Wright: Used to design, upgrade, and manufacture vehicles.
