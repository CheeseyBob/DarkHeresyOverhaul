Aspects

Aspects confer bonuses/penalties to types of actions. They can also prevent or allow certain kinds of actions, if it makes sense. Characters can place Aspects on things by taking Create Advantage actions and can remove Aspects by taking Overcome actions.

By default, the bonus/penalty applied by an Aspect is ±10. An Aspect can be upgraded to a higher bonus/penalty, in increments of ±10. When a duplcate of an Aspect is to be created, the existing Aspect is instead upgraded. When an upgraded Aspect is overcome, it is downgraded by one increment instead of being removed. For example, if a character who is Stunned becomes Stunned again, they end up with a single Stunned(20) Aspect. If they then Resist Stun and succeed, they end up merely Stunned and must Resist Stun again to remove the Aspect.

On Fire
Prevents Darkness
Allows If target is a character, at the start of their turn, they take 1d5 damage ignoring Armour and Toughness, upgrading the Aspect on a 5. Any other exposed characters must oppose Ignite at the start of their turn. Each round that the fire is able to consume flammable material, it grows (upgrading the Aspect), otherwise it shrinks (downgrading the Aspect). Exposed objects with AP up to the level of the Aspect take AP damage, becoming On Fire if they are flammable.
Bonus To Ignite
Penalty To Everything except Extinguish Fire.
Overcome After Extinguish Fire.
