Aspects

Aspects confer bonuses/penalties to types of actions. They can also have special effects. Characters can place Aspects on things by taking Create Advantage actions and can remove Aspects by taking Overcome actions.

By default, the bonus/penalty applied by an Aspect is ±10. An Aspect can be upgraded to a higher bonus/penalty, in increments of ±10. When a duplcate of an Aspect is to be created, the existing Aspect is instead upgraded. When an upgraded Aspect is overcome, it is downgraded by one increment instead of being removed. For example, if a character who is Stunned becomes Stunned again, they end up with a single Stunned(2) Aspect. If they successfully Resist Stun, they end up merely Stunned and must Resist Stun again to remove the Aspect.

By default, Create Advantage Actions cannot increase an Aspect's level past the Characteristic Bonus of the Characteristic used for the Test. In addition to this, Aspects cannot be upgraded past level 10.

Rule of thumb:

  • Bonuses/penalties apply only to the target. Any benefits other characters get against the target should be come from penalites to the target opposing that action (and vice versa). The exception to this is when the Aspect is providing a static difficulty to something, for example a Concealed character or a Locked container simply makes it harder to Notice or Open rather than actively opposing.
  • The target of an aspect should get one opportunity to oppose the effects of an Aspect per round, including one before the effect is applied (either at the start of their turn or when opposing the creation of the Aspect).
AL = Aspect Level

Common Aspects for Characters

Bleeding Out
Bonus To -
Penalty To All Tests
Special The target makes a Toughness Test once per turn. On failure, this aspect is upgraded. When AL exceeds double the target's TB, they die.
Overcome After Rest

Burning
Bonus To -
Penalty To -
Special Prevents effects of Darkness up to ALx1m away. The target takes 1d10+AL Energy damage once per turn. If the target dies, they create an area of Fire.
Overcome After Extinguish Fire (Agility)

Concealed
Bonus To -
Penalty To Notice or Search vs target
Special The target can only be detected with a successful Notice or Search.
Overcome After Target leaves their place of concealment

Dazzled
Bonus To -
Penalty To Anything requiring sight:
Weapon Skill, Ballistic Skill, Agility and Perception Tests.
Special -
Overcome After Resist (Physically), once per turn

Disguised
Bonus To Deceive (when the deception involves the disguise)
Penalty To Read Person or Inspect vs target
Special Characters think the target is who they are disguised as unless they successfully Read Person or Inspect.
Overcome After Target removes the disguise

Encumbered
Bonus To -
Penalty To Anything made harder by restricted movement:
Weapon Skill, Ballistic Skill and Agility Tests.
Special -
Overcome After Removing the encumbering item.

Fatigued
Bonus To -
Penalty To Anything requiring physical exhertion:
Weapon Skill, Strength and Agility Tests.
Special -
Overcome After Consuming Food (Long Action Toughness Test)

Frenzied
Bonus To Anything that benefits from furiosity:
Weapon Skill and Strength Tests
Penalty To Anything requiring clear thought, patience or precision:
Ballistic Skill, Intelligence, Perception and Fellowship Tests
Special Prevents target becoming Intimidated Frightened, Pinned or Stunned.
Overcome After Resist (Mentally), once per turn

Frightened
Bonus To -
Penalty To Anything requiring composure:
Weapon Skill, Ballistic Skill, Strength, Intelligence, Perception, Willpower and Fellowship Tests.
Special -
Overcome After Resist (Mentally), once per turn

Grappled
Bonus To -
Penalty To Anything made harder while grappled:
Weapon Skill, Ballistic Skill and Agility Tests
Special Allows the grappler to use Strength to Attack (Melee), Stun or disarm, and to oppose physical actions target takes.
Overcome After Grapple (Break) or the grappler lets go.

Inspired
Bonus To Anything improved with confidence:
Willpower and Fellowship Tests
Penalty To -
Special -
Overcome After Becoming Intimidated, Frightened or Pinned; automatically, once per hour.

Intimidated
Bonus To -
Penalty To Any hostile action against the intimidator.
Special -
Overcome After Resist (Mentally), once per turn

Pinned
Bonus To -
Penalty To -
Special The target cannot stay in or move into the open in the line of fire of the suppressor.

Prone

Use Cover; Conceal (Self); etc.
